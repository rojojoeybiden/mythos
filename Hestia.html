Hestia


In Greek mythology, Hestia is the virgin goddess of the hearth, home, and family. She is also the goddess of the sacrificial flame and the protector of the home and state. Hestia is one of the Twelve Olympians, the major deities of the Greek pantheon.

In addition to her role as the virgin goddess of the hearth, home, and family, Hestia is revered as the guardian of the sacrificial flame and protector of both the household and the state. As one of the Twelve Olympians, the principal deities in Greek mythology, she holds a sacred and central position in the divine hierarchy. Hestia's peaceful presence embodies the importance of domesticity, communal harmony, and the sacred flame that symbolizes the essence of the home.
