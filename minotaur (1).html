Minotaur


The Minotaur was a legendary creature from Crete, possessing a human body and a bull's head. It was the offspring of Pasiphae, wife of Minos, and a white bull gifted to Minos by the god Poseidon for sacrifice. Rather than sacrificing the bull, Minos kept it alive, which incurred the wrath of Poseidon. As punishment, Poseidon caused Pasiphae to fall deeply in love with the bull. The offspring of Pasiphae and the bull was confined within the Labyrinth constructed by Daedalus at the behest of Minos.

Following the death of Minos's son, Androgeos, at the hands of Athenians, Minos sought revenge. He demanded that seven Athenian youths and seven maidens be sent every ninth year (or in some versions, every year) to be devoured by the Minotaur. In response, the Athenian hero Theseus volunteered to be part of the tribute. With the assistance of Ariadne, Minos and Pasiphae's daughter, Theseus successfully navigated the Labyrinth and slayed the Minotaur, thus ending the sacrificial tribute. Theseus, aided by Ariadne, managed to escape Crete.
