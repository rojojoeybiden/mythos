Cerberus


Cerberus is a three-headed dog that serves as the gatekeeper of the underworld. He is aptly referred to as the hound of Hades. Cerberus is part of the twelve labors of Heracles, the task Heracles had to bring the beast to Eurystheus. Cerberus is an iconic feature in some forms of modern media, taking the form of a guard or a trial the protagonists must face.

The earliest mentions of Cerberus were in the Illiad and the Odyssey. According to Hesiod, Cerberus is one of the offsprings of Echidna and Typhon and had fifty heads, Pindar gave him a hundred heads and many other philosophers and writers throughout the ages would write different versions of Cerberus. All having features like a single head, snakes for tails and much more.
