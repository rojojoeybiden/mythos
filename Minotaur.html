Minotaur


Born from the unnatural union between Queen Pasiphaë and a sacred white bull sent by Poseidon, the Minotaur possessed a humanoid body coupled with a bull's head and tail. Also known as Asterion, this monstrous creature found its infamous residence within the labyrinth on the island of Crete. The labyrinth, constructed by King Minos, was intended to confine the Minotaur, a living consequence of divine punishment or curse believed to be associated with the sins of Minos or Queen Pasiphaë.

The Minotaur's existence demanded dark rituals, as human sacrifices were regularly sent into the labyrinth to satisfy its hunger. The hero Theseus, with the assistance of Princess Ariadne, undertook the perilous task of navigating the intricate maze. Ultimately, Theseus confronted and defeated the Minotaur, liberating Athens from the grisly necessity of periodic sacrifices and marking the end of the creature's reign of terror.
